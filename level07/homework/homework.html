The First Computer

The first computer was created in 1943. It was called the ENIAC. A group of engineers in the United States built it during World War II.
The computer was very big, as big as a room, and it used thousands of switches and wires. It was made to help with calculations for the army. The ENIAC could do math much faster than people.

ENIAC

The ENIAC was very different from today's computers. It did not have a screen, keyboard, or mouse. People had to use many switches and buttons to make it work.
The computer could only do one task at a time, and it used a lot of electricity. Even though it was not as smart as modern computers, the was a big step forward.
It showed that machines could solve problems faster than people. This was the beginning of the computer age.
